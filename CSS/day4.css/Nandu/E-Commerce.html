E-Commerce

-commerce, or electronic commerce, refers to the buying and selling of goods and services over the internet. It encompasses all online commercial transactions, including business-to-business (B2B) and business-to-consumer (B2C) activities, and relies on platforms like websites, mobile apps, and social media. E-commerce has revolutionized the way businesses operate and consumers shop, offering enhanced convenience and accessibility. 
Key aspects of e-commerce:
Buying and selling online:
E-commerce involves the online exchange of products and services, facilitated by electronic platforms. 
Types of transactions:
E-commerce includes various transaction types, such as B2B (business selling to another business), B2C (business selling to consumers), and C2C (consumer selling to consumer). 
Platforms:
E-commerce relies on platforms like websites, mobile apps, online marketplaces (e.g., Amazon, eBay), and social media. 
Benefits:
E-commerce offers benefits like increased reach, reduced costs, enhanced convenience, and personalized shopping experiences for both businesses and consumers. 
Evolution:
E-commerce has evolved from simple online retail to a complex ecosystem of online marketplaces, subscription services, and B2B platforms. 
Impact:
E-commerce has transformed the retail landscape, influencing how businesses operate and how consumers shop globally.
